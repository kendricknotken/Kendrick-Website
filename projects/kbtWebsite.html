Design Process and Choices

Understanding my audience

  • I was tasked with creating a website for a less technologically savvy audience
  • My goal was to make a better-looking website that was also more accessible to a larger audience of people
  • The website should be able to allow users to easily find blogs to read, books to purchase, and research to study

Navigation

After Beta testing the site with users in the 50+ age demographic, I asked for feedback and there was trend with regards to navigation being difficult.
The reason they found it difficult was because they didn’t recognize what they could or could not click on.

This included:

  • Symbols or Icons, such as the sidebar, the three bars, in the top left corner of the screen
  • Clickable Images
  • Clickable Text


My solution for this issue was to add a quick links section at the bottom of all pages



The reason I added this section at the bottom of the page was because I wanted it out of the way from people who didn’t need the extra assistance, but it was there for people who needed it. In addition to this section I also added an Assistance Page which could be accessed by clicking the Help link under Quick Links.



Some blogs would include references so I used ID Tags to allow a user to easily find the reference to what they were reading

Formatting

Breathing Room

When I first added the Latest Releases section to the sidebar it did not have a drop down option but I decided to add one because this new section made the sidebar crowded.

  • What made the sidebar crowded were the pictures to I gave users the ability to remove them if they choose
  • The Latest Releases tab is helpful because it lets frequent visitors of the site know what is new
  • However after a user views the content and decides they don't need it anymore, they have to either close the sidebar completely or have it be in their Field of View the whole time they’re on the website.
  • In my personal experience, I enjoy having the sidebar open while a scroll so I wanted the sidebar not be so distracting

My solution, as I stated earlier, was to add a drop down menu option so that users could either open or close the tab



Drawing Attention

My Mom , the client, always told me her Media page was the least visited page and even when people do visit it, it is doesn't recieve many interations

I think this was partily in reason that:

  • The Media tab had links to other bodies of work, but it didn’t say about what
  • Leaving much of the links as unknown, people didn't know what they were clicking on and would ultimately be guessing about what content layed ahead


My solution for this issue was to add short summaries under the links. Now if users were interested in learning more about a certain topic they would have an easier time finding it; no more guessing >:D
